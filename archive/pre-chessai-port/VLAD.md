### As White — Italian Cage with Spanish Styling

**The Coiled Spring setup — non-negotiable move order:**

```
1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4
4.c3 (prepares d4, restricts Black)
5.d3 (solid base, no early d4 overcommitment)
6.a4 (cramps queenside, creates bishop retreat square)
7.O-O
8.h3 (prevents ...Bg4 pin)
9.Re1
```

4. **High-velocity sacrifice** — Bxh6
5. **Central explosion** — d4 break

**The Philosophy:** Feign quiet positional play. Induce opponent over-extension. Cage is built. Spring is coiled. Then strike.

## 🚨 Confirmed Weaknesses (Priority Drill Targets)

### #1 — The "Move 9 Speed Trap" ⚠️ CRITICAL
**What happens:** As the center opens around move 9–10, natural fast playing speed returns. Result: queen blundered to knight forks or rooks on open files.
**Root cause:** Discipline failure, not tactical blindness. The pattern is known, the habit overrides it.
**Vlad's prescription:** Mandatory 30–60 second pause before ANY move from move 8 onward. Ask "Is my queen safe?" out loud before touching a piece. Non-negotiable.

### #2 — Panic Simplification
**What happens:** When opponent plays fast ...c5/...d5 breaks, tendency to release tension via early pawn exchanges or trading active knights.
**Root cause:** Fear response. The "Coiled Spring" gets released prematurely.
**Vlad's prescription:** When opponent attacks, ask "Does this move actually require a response?" Hold the tension. The cage is the advantage.

### #3 — Pawn-Pushing vs. Early Queen Sorties
**What happens:** When opponent plays ...Qg5 or ...Qh4, reflex is to push g3/g4/h3 in front of king.
**Root cause:** Pattern mismatch — treating queen aggression as a pawn problem.
**Vlad's prescription:** The rule is simple: **Develop and Castle.** Queen sorties early are a trap. Don't walk into it. Develop a piece, castle, queen has no target.

### #4 — Missing Forcing Moves in Winning Positions
**What happens:** When already winning massively, occasionally misses the most forcing continuation (e.g. missing 24.Bxf7+).
**Root cause:** Relaxing calculation when ahead.
**Vlad's prescription:** The game isn't over until it's over. Run the **CCT check** (Checks, Captures, Threats) before EVERY move, especially when winning.

## 🧠 The 4-Step Master Mental Loop

Before every single move, execute in order:

```
1. OPPONENT'S INTENT    — What does my opponent want? Is my queen safe?
2. CCT CHECK            — What are all Checks, Captures, and Threats available?
3. LAZY PIECE UPGRADE   — What is my worst piece? Can this move improve it?
4. PRE-MOVE VERIFY      — After I move, can they take anything for free?
```

**Time requirement:** Moves 1–8: normal pace. Move 9+: minimum 30–60 second pause enforced.
